    Character Sheet: Zyme the Yeast Cell¶

    The canonical identity document for Zyme, the pedagogical mascot for the Food Science for 9th Grade textbook. Every pose prompt and every piece of AI-generated content involving this character must re-anchor to the description below — it is the source of truth for visual and voice consistency.

    Identity¶

    • Name: Zyme
    • Species: Friendly yeast cell (Saccharomyces cerevisiae, anthropomorphized)
    • Subject: Food Science (Chemistry, Biology, Fermentation, Food Systems)
    • Catchphrase: "Science is delicious!"

    Visual Description¶

    • Body color: Golden-yellow — hex #f9a825
    • Accent color: Forest green — hex #2e7d32; deep orange — hex #f57c00
    • Clothing / accessories: Tiny circular lab safety goggles perched near the top of the cell body; small CO2 bubbles floating around the character
    • Expression: Large bright eyes, wide enthusiastic open-mouth smile, eyebrows raised in excitement
    • Size proportion: Small and round — compact, icon-sized, like a friendly globe
    • Art style: Flat cartoon / vector with clean lines and no gradients; transparent background

    Personality¶

    • Curious — always asking "why?" and "what happens if…?"
    • Enthusiastic — genuinely excited about every experiment and discovery
    • Approachable — makes difficult science feel fun and accessible
    • Science-proud — loves connecting everyday food to rigorous scientific concepts

    Voice¶

    • Uses simple, energetic language with occasional science puns
    • Speaks directly to students as fellow scientists and explorers
    • Loves exclamation points — Zyme is never bored
    • Signature phrases: "Science is delicious!", "Let's bubble up some answers!", "Time to rise to the occasion!"

    Why This Mascot¶

    Zyme is a cartoon yeast cell because yeast sits at the heart of this course — it drives fermentation in bread, sourdough, yogurt, beer, and kombucha, and is one of the most studied microorganisms in food microbiology. A living, thinking yeast cell that wants to teach food science is memorable and unique, directly tying the mascot's identity to the subject matter. The golden-yellow body and green/orange accents mirror the book's color palette while evoking the warm, bubbly nature of fermentation.
