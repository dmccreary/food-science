# List of MicroSims for Food Science

Interactive Micro Simulations to help 9th-grade students learn food science
fundamentals — from molecules and microbes to baking, nutrition, and
sustainable food systems. Each MicroSim focuses on a single concept and is
embedded into chapters via iframes.

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **[Bacteria Cell Structure Explorer](./bacteria-cell-anatomy/index.md)**

    ![Bacteria Cell Structure Explorer](./bacteria-cell-anatomy/bacteria-cell-anatomy.png)

    Click on each labeled part of a bacterial cell to learn its name and function. Identifies the key structures that make microbes unique.

-   **[Bacterial Growth Curve Simulation](./bacterial-growth-curve/index.md)**

    ![Bacterial Growth Curve Simulation](./bacterial-growth-curve/bacterial-growth-curve.png)

    Adjust temperature and nutrients to see how bacterial populations move through the lag, log, stationary, and death phases of growth.

-   **[Carbohydrate Size Ladder — From Sugar to Starch](./carbohydrate-size-ladder/index.md)**

    ![Carbohydrate Size Ladder](./carbohydrate-size-ladder/carbohydrate-size-ladder.png)

    Climb the ladder from single sugars (monosaccharides) up to disaccharides and starches, showing how small units link into larger carbohydrates.

-   **[Carbon Footprint of Common Foods](./food-carbon-footprint-chart/index.md)**

    ![Carbon Footprint of Common Foods](./food-carbon-footprint-chart/food-carbon-footprint-chart.png)

    Interactive Chart.js bar chart comparing the greenhouse-gas emissions of common foods, revealing which choices have the largest environmental impact.

-   **[Data Graphing Lab](./data-graphing-lab/index.md)**

    ![Data Graphing Lab](./data-graphing-lab/data-graphing-lab.png)

    Build and interpret bar graphs and line graphs from real food science data sets. Practice the core scientific skill of turning numbers into insight.

-   **[Digestive System Nutrient Journey](./digestive-system-journey/index.md)**

    ![Digestive System Nutrient Journey](./digestive-system-journey/digestive-system-journey.png)

    Follow a bite of food through each organ of the digestive system and watch how carbohydrates, proteins, and fats are broken down and absorbed.

-   **[Experimental Variables Identifier](./variables-identifier/index.md)**

    ![Experimental Variables Identifier](./variables-identifier/variables-identifier.png)

    Read a food science experiment scenario and classify each factor as the independent, dependent, or controlled variable.

-   **[Freezing Rate and Ice Crystal Size](./ice-crystal-freezing-sim/index.md)**

    ![Freezing Rate and Ice Crystal Size](./ice-crystal-freezing-sim/ice-crystal-freezing-sim.png)

    Compare slow home freezing to fast commercial freezing and see why crystal size determines whether your strawberry stays plump or turns to mush.

-   **[Gluten Network Development](./gluten-network-formation/index.md)**

    ![Gluten Network Development](./gluten-network-formation/gluten-network-formation.png)

    Watch glutenin and gliadin proteins link into the stretchy network that gives bread its structure. Adjust mixing time and protein content to see the effect.

-   **[Grilled Cheese Science Timeline](./grilled-cheese-timeline/index.md)**

    ![Grilled Cheese Science Timeline](./grilled-cheese-timeline/grilled-cheese-timeline.png)

    A vis-timeline walkthrough of every chemical and physical change that happens while a grilled cheese cooks — conduction, melting, Maillard browning, and more.

-   **[Hydroponic Project Data Dashboard](./hydroponic-growth-dashboard/index.md)**

    ![Hydroponic Project Data Dashboard](./hydroponic-growth-dashboard/hydroponic-growth-dashboard.png)

    A Chart.js dashboard for tracking plant height, pH, and nutrient levels in a class hydroponic experiment over time.

-   **[Hydroponic System Types Comparison](./hydroponic-system-types/index.md)**

    ![Hydroponic System Types Comparison](./hydroponic-system-types/hydroponic-system-types.png)

    Compare the four main hydroponic system types (wick, deep water culture, NFT, ebb-and-flow) and choose the best fit for a given budget, space, and crop.

-   **[Interactive Molecule Builder](./molecule-builder/index.md)**

    ![Interactive Molecule Builder](./molecule-builder/molecule-builder.png)

    Snap together atoms to build water, sugar, and other key food molecules — and see how bonding creates compounds with properties unlike the elements that form them.

-   **[Interactive pH Food Scale](./ph-food-scale/index.md)**

    ![Interactive pH Food Scale](./ph-food-scale/ph-food-scale.png)

    Place common foods on a pH scale from lemon juice to baking soda. Explore how pH connects to sour taste, safety, and food preservation.

-   **[Learning Graph Viewer](./graph-viewer/index.md)**

    ![Learning Graph Viewer](./graph-viewer/graph-viewer.png)

    Browse the full concept dependency graph for the course. Search for any concept, filter by category, and trace prerequisite relationships interactively.

-   **[Maillard Reaction vs. Caramelization](./browning-reactions-explorer/index.md)**

    ![Maillard Reaction vs. Caramelization](./browning-reactions-explorer/browning-reactions-explorer.png)

    Step through the two great browning reactions side by side. Compare reactants, temperatures, and the very different flavors they produce.

-   **[Microwave vs. Conventional Oven Heating](./microwave-vs-oven-heat/index.md)**

    ![Microwave vs. Conventional Oven Heating](./microwave-vs-oven-heat/microwave-vs-oven-heat.png)

    See exactly where heat is generated inside a piece of food in a microwave versus a conventional oven, and why the two cook so differently.

-   **[Multisensory Flavor Perception Map](./multisensory-flavor-builder/index.md)**

    ![Multisensory Flavor Perception Map](./multisensory-flavor-builder/multisensory-flavor-builder.png)

    Toggle taste, smell, sight, sound, and texture inputs to see how each sense combines to produce the unified experience we call "flavor."

-   **[NOVA Classification Sorter](./nova-classification-sorter/index.md)**

    ![NOVA Classification Sorter](./nova-classification-sorter/nova-classification-sorter.png)

    Drag foods into NOVA groups 1 through 4 and learn what really separates whole foods from ultra-processed products.

-   **[Nutrient Deficiency Explorer](./nutrient-deficiency-map/index.md)**

    ![Nutrient Deficiency Explorer](./nutrient-deficiency-map/nutrient-deficiency-map.png)

    Match each micronutrient to the deficiency disease it prevents and the foods that supply it — vitamin C and scurvy, iron and anemia, and more.

-   **[Peptide Bond Formation Animator](./peptide-bond-animator/index.md)**

    ![Peptide Bond Formation Animator](./peptide-bond-animator/peptide-bond-animator.png)

    Step through the condensation reaction that joins two amino acids into a peptide, releasing a water molecule. The first step toward building any protein.

-   **[pH Scale Explorer](./ph-scale-explorer/index.md)**

    ![pH Scale Explorer](./ph-scale-explorer/ph-scale-explorer.png)

    Place common foods on the pH scale and connect acidity to taste — sour foods are acidic, bitter foods often basic, and water sits in the middle.

-   **[Protein Source Comparison](./protein-sources-comparison/index.md)**

    ![Protein Source Comparison](./protein-sources-comparison/protein-sources-comparison.png)

    Compare beef, chicken, fish, tofu, beans, and insects across protein content, land use, water use, and emissions to evaluate real-world food tradeoffs.

-   **[Saturated vs. Unsaturated Fat Explorer](./fat-molecule-explorer/index.md)**

    ![Saturated vs. Unsaturated Fat Explorer](./fat-molecule-explorer/fat-molecule-explorer.png)

    Compare straight saturated fatty acid chains against kinked unsaturated chains and discover why butter is solid while olive oil is liquid.

-   **[Scientific Method Steps Explorer](./scientific-method-explorer/index.md)**

    ![Scientific Method Steps Explorer](./scientific-method-explorer/scientific-method-explorer.png)

    Click through the seven steps of the scientific method using food science examples, from asking a question to communicating results.

-   **[Sourdough Rise-and-Fall Cycle](./sourdough-rise-fall-cycle/index.md)**

    ![Sourdough Rise-and-Fall Cycle](./sourdough-rise-fall-cycle/sourdough-rise-fall-cycle.png)

    Track a sourdough starter as it rises, peaks, and collapses. Learn to read the cycle so you can time your bake to peak yeast activity.

-   **[Temperature Danger Zone Visualizer](./temperature-danger-zone/index.md)**

    ![Temperature Danger Zone Visualizer](./temperature-danger-zone/temperature-danger-zone.png)

    Move food through cold storage, the 40–140 °F danger zone, and safe cooking temperatures. See how time and temperature drive bacterial growth.

-   **[Three Modes of Heat Transfer in Cooking](./heat-transfer-cooking-explorer/index.md)**

    ![Three Modes of Heat Transfer in Cooking](./heat-transfer-cooking-explorer/heat-transfer-cooking-explorer.png)

    Identify conduction, convection, and radiation in real cooking scenarios — and see at the molecular level what makes each one tick.

-   **[Water as Universal Solvent](./water-solvent-explorer/index.md)**

    ![Water as Universal Solvent](./water-solvent-explorer/water-solvent-explorer.png)

    Drop polar, ionic, and nonpolar substances into water and watch which ones dissolve. The molecular reason oil and water just won't mix.

-   **[What Happens Inside a Loaf as It Bakes](./bread-baking-timeline/index.md)**

    ![What Happens Inside a Loaf as It Bakes](./bread-baking-timeline/bread-baking-timeline.png)

    A timeline of the chemical and physical changes inside a loaf of bread as the oven temperature climbs — oven spring, starch gelation, crust browning, and more.

-   **[World Map of Fermented Foods](./world-fermented-foods-map/index.md)**

    ![World Map of Fermented Foods](./world-fermented-foods-map/world-fermented-foods-map.png)

    A Leaflet world map of fermented food traditions — kimchi, miso, kefir, sauerkraut, injera, and more — grouped by the fermentation pathway each uses.

</div>
