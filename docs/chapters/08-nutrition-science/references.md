# References: Nutrition Science — What Food Does for Your Body

1. [Nutrition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutrition) - Wikipedia - Broad overview of nutritional science covering macronutrients, micronutrients, dietary requirements, digestion, metabolism, and current research on diet and chronic disease prevention.

2. [Digestion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digestion) - Wikipedia - Detailed article on the digestive process from mouth to large intestine including mechanical and chemical digestion, enzyme roles, nutrient absorption mechanisms, and the enteric nervous system.

3. [Gut microbiota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gut_microbiota) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive coverage of the human gut microbiome including species composition, functions (fermentation of fiber, immune modulation, vitamin synthesis), and connections to health and disease.

4. Understanding Nutrition (15th Edition) - Eleanor Noss Whitney, Sharon Rady Rolfes - Cengage Learning - Leading undergraduate nutrition textbook with excellent chapters on macronutrient digestion, DRI tables, energy balance, Nutrition Facts labels, and evaluating dietary claims.

5. Nutrition: Science and Applications (3rd Edition) - Lori A. Smolin, Mary B. Grosvenor - Wiley - Accessible college nutrition text connecting biochemical mechanisms of nutrient metabolism to practical dietary guidelines; suitable as a teacher reference for expanding chapter depth.

6. [USDA MyPlate](https://www.myplate.gov/) - USDA Center for Nutrition Policy and Promotion - Official U.S. dietary guidelines resource with food group visual tools, nutrient information, and lesson plans aligned to school nutrition education; supports the label-reading lab activity.

7. [Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health: The Nutrition Source](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource/) - Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health - Evidence-based nutrition information on macronutrients, healthy eating plates, debunking nutrition myths, and current research on diet and chronic disease.

8. [NIH MedlinePlus: Nutrition](https://medlineplus.gov/nutrition.html) - National Institutes of Health - Curated collection of reliable nutrition science articles from NIH agencies covering vitamins, minerals, dietary guidelines, and how nutrients support body systems.

9. [Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics: EatRight](https://www.eatright.org/food/nutrition) - Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics - Professional dietetics organization's public-facing nutrition resources; provides science-backed information on macronutrients, reading Nutrition Facts labels, and common nutrition myths to evaluate.

10. [Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2020–2025](https://www.dietaryguidelines.gov/) - U.S. Department of Agriculture and U.S. Department of Health and Human Services - Official federal dietary recommendations with supporting evidence summaries; directly supports the school cafeteria menu evaluation and label-reading activities.
