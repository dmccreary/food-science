# References: Sourdough and Wild Fermentation

1. [Sourdough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sourdough) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive article on sourdough history, microbiology of wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria communities, starter creation and maintenance, and how fermentation time and temperature shape flavor.

2. [Lactic acid bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lactic_acid_bacteria) - Wikipedia - Explains the genera involved in sourdough (Lactobacillus, Leuconostoc, Pediococcus), their metabolic pathways (homofermentative vs. heterofermentative), and the acids they produce.

3. [Saccharomyces cerevisiae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saccharomyces_cerevisiae) - Wikipedia - Detailed article on the primary wild yeast in sourdough starters; covers cell biology, fermentation metabolism, carbon dioxide production, and how temperature affects yeast activity.

4. The Art of Fermentation: An In-Depth Exploration of Essential Concepts and Processes from Around the World - Sandor Ellix Katz - Chelsea Green Publishing - Encyclopedic guide to fermentation including wild yeast capture, sourdough starter ecology, and the cultural history of fermented foods; excellent for project background reading.

5. Modernist Bread (Volume 1: History and Fundamentals) - Nathan Myhrvold and Francisco Migoya - The Cooking Lab - Definitive scientific reference on bread; Chapter 3 covers sourdough microbiology in extraordinary depth including starter microbiome analysis, pH curves, and hydration effects on organic acid production.

6. [The Perfect Loaf: How to Make a Sourdough Starter](https://www.theperfectloaf.com/guides/how-to-make-sourdough-starter/) - The Perfect Loaf - Illustrated guide to creating and maintaining a sourdough starter with daily photographs, troubleshooting tips, and scientific explanations of each stage of the 1:1:1 feeding cycle.

7. [King Arthur Baking: Sourdough Starter](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/learn/guides/sourdough/introduction) - King Arthur Baking Company - Comprehensive starter guide including float test instructions, visual health indicators, feeding schedules, and how hydration and temperature affect fermentation pace.

8. [Full Proof Baking: The Science of Sourdough](https://www.youtube.com/@fullproofbaking) - Full Proof Baking (YouTube) - Science-forward video series explaining bulk fermentation, alveograph structure, scoring, and bake outcomes with rigorous controlled comparisons students can observe and discuss.

9. [Cultures for Health: Sourdough Starter](https://www.culturesforhealth.com/learn/sourdough/creating-sourdough-starter/) - Cultures for Health - Student-accessible guide to starting and maintaining a sourdough culture, with explanations of why discard and feeding are necessary to prevent acid toxicity and maintain a healthy yeast-bacteria balance.

10. [MicrobeWorld: The Microbiology of Sourdough](https://www.microbeworld.org/podcasts) - American Society for Microbiology - Podcast episodes and articles on the sourdough microbiome including research on regional differences in wild yeast populations and how starter geography shapes flavor.
