# References: The Molecules of Food

1. [Macromolecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macromolecule) - Wikipedia - Overview of the four classes of biological macromolecules — carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, and nucleic acids — with structural diagrams and food relevance. Core foundation for this chapter.

2. [Carbohydrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbohydrate) - Wikipedia - Detailed coverage of monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides including starch, cellulose, and glycogen. Explains glycosidic bonds and why structure determines digestibility.

3. [Protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive article on amino acid structure, peptide bonds, protein folding, and the four levels of protein structure. Explains how protein shape determines function in food.

4. Food Chemistry (4th Edition) - H.-D. Belitz, W. Grosch, P. Schieberle - Springer - The gold-standard reference on food chemistry; chapters on carbohydrates, proteins, and lipids provide rigorous molecular explanations for every transformation covered in this course.

5. Biochemistry (8th Edition) - Jeremy M. Berg, John L. Tymoczko, Gregory J. Gatto Jr. - W. H. Freeman - Standard undergraduate biochemistry text; chapters on amino acids, carbohydrate structure, and lipid chemistry provide the molecular-level detail supporting this chapter.

6. [Khan Academy: Biological Macromolecules](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/ap-biology/chemistry-of-life/macromolecules/a/macromolecules-overview) - Khan Academy - Free video lessons and practice on all four macromolecule classes with clear structural diagrams and monomer-polymer animations. Excellent for visual learners.

7. [CK-12: Proteins](https://www.ck-12.org/biology/proteins/) - CK-12 Foundation - Free adaptive textbook chapter on protein structure and function with interactive diagrams; reading level matched to high school students.

8. [Lumen Learning: Biological Macromolecules](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/suny-wmopen-biology1/chapter/biological-molecules/) - Lumen Learning - Open-access college biology module with clear explanations of carbohydrate, protein, and lipid chemistry including functional group identification.

9. [USDA FoodData Central](https://fdc.nal.usda.gov/) - USDA - Comprehensive nutritional database for thousands of foods; students can look up macronutrient and micronutrient content of specific foods to apply molecular concepts to real examples.

10. [Water Activity and Food Safety](https://www.ift.org/news-and-publications/food-technology-magazine/issues/2021/october/columns/beyond-the-basics-water-activity) - Institute of Food Technologists - Explains the concept of water activity (aw) and how it determines food preservation and safety; connects Chapter 2 water chemistry to food shelf life.
