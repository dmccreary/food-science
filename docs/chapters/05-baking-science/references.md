# References: Baking Science — Gluten, Leavening, and Bread

1. [Gluten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluten) - Wikipedia - Explains the protein composition of gluten (glutenin and gliadin), network formation during hydration and kneading, and how gluten structure affects bread texture and elasticity.

2. [Leavening agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leavening_agent) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of biological, chemical, and physical leavening agents including yeast CO₂ production, baking soda/acid reactions, and steam formation in oven-spring.

3. [Bread](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bread) - Wikipedia - Extensive article on the history, chemistry, and types of bread worldwide; covers ingredient functions, fermentation, baking temperatures, and crust formation including Maillard browning.

4. Bread Science: The Chemistry and Craft of Making Bread - Emily Buehler - Two Blue Books - Accessible, illustrated guide to the science of bread-making specifically written for curious bakers; explains gluten, fermentation, and shaping in clear molecular terms for non-specialists.

5. The Bread Baker's Apprentice (15th Anniversary Edition) - Peter Reinhart - Ten Speed Press - Classic bread-baking text with scientific explanations of pre-ferments, gluten development, fermentation stages, and oven dynamics; widely used in high school and college baking science courses.

6. [King Arthur Baking: The Science of Baking](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/learn/resources/baking-science) - King Arthur Baking Company - Extensive free library of science-focused baking articles explaining ingredient roles, flour protein content, and chemical leavening with detailed photos and step-by-step labs.

7. [Serious Eats: The Science of Bread](https://www.seriouseats.com/science-bread-gluten-development) - Serious Eats - In-depth science articles on gluten development, hydration ratios, and oven spring with controlled experiments comparing different flour types and kneading methods.

8. [Bob's Red Mill: Baking Science](https://www.bobsredmill.com/blog/baking-tips/leavening-agents-in-baking/) - Bob's Red Mill - Clear explanation of chemical leavening agents (baking soda vs. baking powder) with chemical equations and practical tips on when to use each; includes double-acting baking powder chemistry.

9. [The Fresh Loaf: Beginner's Bread Baking Guide](https://www.thefreshloaf.com/handbook) - The Fresh Loaf - Community baking resource with science-based guides on flour selection, hydration, fermentation timing, and troubleshooting; supports the bread-baking lab activity.

10. [Science Notes: Chemistry of Bread Baking](https://sciencenotes.org/chemistry-of-bread-baking/) - Science Notes - Concise summary of the chemical reactions in bread making including yeast fermentation, Maillard reaction, and starch gelatinization; ideal as a pre-lab reading assignment.
