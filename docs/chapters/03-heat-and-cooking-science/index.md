# Chapter 3: Heat, Cooking Science, and Chemical Reactions

## Summary

This chapter answers one of food science's most fundamental questions: what actually
happens to food when you cook it? Students explore the three modes of heat transfer
(conduction, convection, radiation), then follow the heat into the food itself —
denaturing proteins, gelatinizing starches, triggering the Maillard reaction and
caramelization, and driving oxidation and browning reactions. The chapter closes with
specialized cooking techniques including pressure cooking, microwave heating, and
emulsification, building the foundation for baking science in Chapter 5.
