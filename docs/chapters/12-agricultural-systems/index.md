# Chapter 12: Agricultural Systems and Sustainability

## Summary

Every food on the plate started on a farm, a ranch, or in the water — and the choices made in that system shape nutrition, environment, and economics globally. This chapter compares conventional and organic farming, examines the role of soil health and biodiversity in food production, and traces how post-harvest handling affects the nutritional quality of fresh produce by the time it reaches consumers. Students analyze sustainable agriculture practices, the water and energy footprints of different food systems, and the science of food miles and carbon emissions.
