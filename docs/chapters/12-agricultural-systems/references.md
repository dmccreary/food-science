# References: Agricultural Systems and Sustainability

1. [Sustainable agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sustainable_agriculture) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of sustainable farming practices including crop rotation, integrated pest management, soil health, water conservation, and the principles of reducing environmental impact while maintaining productivity.

2. [Organic farming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_farming) - Wikipedia - Detailed comparison of organic and conventional farming methods including prohibited synthetic inputs, soil amendment practices, certification standards, and the scientific evidence on nutritional differences.

3. [Food miles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_miles) - Wikipedia - Explains the concept of food miles, how transport distance is calculated, carbon emissions from different transport modes, and the debate about whether food miles alone accurately capture environmental impact.

4. Sustainable Food Systems: Building a New Paradigm - Terry Marsden, Adrian Morley - Routledge - Examines global and local food system design from environmental, economic, and equity perspectives; provides context for comparing the carbon footprint of different agricultural and distribution choices.

5. Soil Science Simplified (5th Edition) - Helmut Kohnke, D.P. Franzmeier - Waveland Press - Accessible soil science text covering soil formation, organic matter, nutrient cycles, and microbial communities; supports the soil health and crop rotation content of this chapter.

6. [USDA National Organic Program](https://www.ams.usda.gov/about-ams/programs-offices/national-organic-program) - USDA Agricultural Marketing Service - Official standards and certification requirements for organic food labeling in the U.S.; explains what "certified organic" means scientifically and legally.

7. [Rodale Institute: Research on Organic Farming](https://rodaleinstitute.org/science/farming-systems-trial/) - Rodale Institute - Results from the longest-running side-by-side comparison of organic and conventional farming systems; provides data on soil health, yields, water quality, and carbon sequestration that supports the chapter's analysis activities.

8. [World Resources Institute: Creating a Sustainable Food Future](https://www.wri.org/research/creating-sustainable-food-future) - World Resources Institute - Comprehensive 2019 report on feeding 10 billion people sustainably by 2050; covers land use, water, emissions, food loss, and dietary shifts with accessible summaries and data visualizations.

9. [Environmental Working Group: Food Scores Database](https://www.ewg.org/foodscores/) - Environmental Working Group - Database rating food products on nutrition, ingredient concerns, and environmental impact; students can use it to analyze the environmental footprint of specific foods in the carbon-footprint lab.

10. [FAO: Post-Harvest Losses and Food Waste](https://www.fao.org/food-loss-and-food-waste/en/) - Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations - Data and resources on post-harvest food losses globally; supports analysis of how crop rotation, storage science, and supply chain decisions affect total food system sustainability.
